Question Types

It is often useful to identify the type of question you are answering. Each type is approached in its own way.

Main Idea: These questions have to do with the main point of a whole passage. They should be done LAST after all other questions are answered.

Main Idea questions can also concern just one paragraph.

Also included in this category are questions that concern the whole passage but are not necessarily a main idea. You may see something like: What is the structure of the passage? You have to work with the whole passage to determine its structure, i.e., chronological order, thesis and examples, etc.

Fact: Fact questions deal with specific details in the passage. The answers may or may not reflect the language of the question—there may be a paraphrase of the idea that is the answer. These questions often have a line reference.

Inference: These questions ask you for information that is not directly stated in the passage. You are looking for the classic “educated guess.” Inference questions also deal with the point of view of authors and characters. Look for specific actions or words that indicate thinking or feeling.

Relationships: These questions deal with cause/effect and comparisons and contrasts in the passage.

Sequence: Sequence questions may ask for chronological events (which happened first, last, etc.) or proper order of sentences in paragraphs or paragraphs in passages.

Vocabulary in context: Vocabulary questions give a line reference and ask what does a particular word mean in a particular context. For example: “In line___ what does the word____ most likely mean?” Vocabulary questions can also ask for the meaning of a phrase or why the author used a particular phrase. Use the context. The correct meaning of the word is the meaning of the word in that context.
