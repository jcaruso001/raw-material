Sentence Fragments

  • A sentence fragment is missing a subject or predicate or both.
  • In order to be complete, a sentence (or independent clause) must contain a subject and a verb and be able to stand alone.
  • Beware of dependent clauses that start with subordinating conjunctions (like if, because, since, and although).
  • A clause that starts with a subordinating conjunction is dependent and cannot stand alone as a sentence, so be sure that you have another clause—an independent one—following the dependent one.
  • If a sentence starts with a dependent clause, always place a comma after it unless it is very short.
  • If the dependent clause comes after the independent clause, the clause can be tested for essentialness. If the dependent clause is essential to the meaning of the sentence, do not place a comma after the independent clause. If the dependent clause is nonessential to the meaning of the sentence, place a comma after the independent clause.
