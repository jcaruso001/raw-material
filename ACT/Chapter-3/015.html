Paragraphs

Some of the multiple-choice questions you’ll see on the English section of the ACT will deal with a paragraph as a whole. In those instances, you’ll be asked to identify sentences that are out of order, sentences that should be rewritten or combined with other sentences, and sentences that should be removed from the paragraph altogether. You’ll also be asked to select appropriate introductory and concluding sentences, transitional words, and supporting examples. You may also be asked to put the sentences of a paragraph or paragraphs of a passage in the correct sequential order. In addition, you could be asked to do one of the following:

  • Choose the phrasing that best represents the goals/point of view of the writer or a character.
  • Change the wording to better reflect a particular meaning intended by the writer.
  • Decide if a sentence (or paragraph) should be removed and identify whey.
  • Decide if a sentence or phrase (or paragraph) should be added an identify why and/or where.
  • Selecta appropriate introductory concluding sentences, transitional words, or supporting examples.
