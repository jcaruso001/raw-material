Graphs of Trigonometric Functions

You will be expected to be able to identify the graph of a trigonometric function from a given equation or to find an equation from a given graph.

The general equation for a given trigonometric function is represented by (shown below for the
sine of angle θ)

y = A sin (B (θ – C)) + D,

where A is the amplitude of the graph (how tall the graph is) and B modifies the period of the graph.
The period of the graph is the distance between one point to its corresponding, repeated point. The period of the graph is 2π/B units.

An increase in A also increases the amplitude or height of the graph (a direct relationship).
Increases in B result in a decrease in the period (an inverse relationship).

A positive value of C shifts the graph C units to the right, while a negative value of C shifts the graph C units to the right. A positive value of D shifts the graph up D units, while a negative value of D shifts the graph D units down.
