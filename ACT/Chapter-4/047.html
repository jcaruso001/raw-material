Right Triangles

A right triangle is a triangle with a right angle. The other angles are, by definition, complementary angles 

The longest side of a right triangle (the one opposite the 90Ëš angle) is called the hypotenuse (c is the hypotenuse in the figure below). The other sides are
often referred to as legs.

Of all the triangles on the exam, right triangles appear the most often. Much information can be obtained from figures that contain right triangles due to the fact that their sides always exist in a particular proportion to each other.
