The Essay

Teaching students to write an essay is not the purpose of this guide. Instead, we will devote the remainder of this chapter to providing you with ACT’s criteria for evaluating the essay you will complete on the writing test. Information about scoring the essay, combined with the skill review provided above, should go a long way in helping you do well on the writing section of the ACT. All the skills covered in the English skills section are applicable to writing your essay.

You will be presented with a prompt that includes:

  • An introductory paragraph explaining an issue to be discussed, and
  • Three perspectives detailing a particular way of thinking about that issue.


Your task is to:

  • Analyze and evaluate the perspectives given;
  • State and develop your own perspective (which may be one of the three);
  • Explain the relationship between your perspective and those given.


You will have 40 minutes to complete the essay. It is very important that you take some time at the beginning of the session to do some thinking about the topic and develop an outline or other graphic organizer. This will be very helpful to you when you actually start wring.

The essay will be scored by two people who will assign a score of 1-6 in four areas. These areas include Ideas and Analysis, Development and Support, Organization, and Language Use and Conventions. The scores will be combined and converted to a scale score.

As stated previously, the Essay is optional, but it could be to your advantage to do it even if your school does not require the essay. This will accomplish the following:

  • It will show your willingness to “go the extra mile.”
  • It will evaluate your proficiency in developing a persuasive essay. Essay writing will be a big part of your college curriculum.
