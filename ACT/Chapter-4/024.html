Solving Algebraic Inequalities

Definitions

An inequality is a statement that compares one quantity or expression to another. Inequalities are
shown using four symbols:

• Greater than: >
4 > 1
1 > -3,
2 > √2

• Greater than or equal to: >
8/3 > 2
2 + 3 > 5

• Less than: <
0 < 4
-6 < -5

• Less than or equal to: ≤
6 - 2 ≤ 5
5/2 ≤ 2.5

Solving inequalities works just like solving an equality, except for one important difference:
When you multiply or divide an inequality by a negative number, the inequality sign flips.

Example 1: Solve the inequality 2x - 5 > 9.

First, add 5 to both sides to get 2x > 14. Then solve for x. The solution x > 7 is the answer. In other words, “all values of x greater than 7” are solutions to this inequality.

Example 2: Solve the inequality 3x + 5 < 5x - 9.

First, subtract 5 from both sides to get 3x < 5x - 14. Then subtract 5x from both sides to get - 2x < - 14. To solve for x, divide both sides by - 2 and switch the inequality sign to get x > 7 as the solution.

You may also be given graphical representations of inequalities.

Example 3: Which of the following algebraic inequalities is represented by the graph below?


A. - 2 > y < 2
B. - 2 < y > 2
C. - 2 ≤ y < 2
D. - 2 < y ≤ 2

The graph represents a line whose solution, y, lies between - 2 and 2. The filled point above -2 shows that y can be equal to -2. The line runs from the filled point to the right. Thus, we know that -2≤ y. The open point above 2 shows that y cannot be equal to 2. The line runs from the open point to the left. Thus, we know that y < 2. To answer the question, you must combine the two inequalities into one statement:
- 2 ≤ y < 2. Therefore, the answer is C.
