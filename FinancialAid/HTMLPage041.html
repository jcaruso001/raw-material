Why I need Financial Support:

When creating a budget for your college years, know that published costs generally include a broad range as students' spending habits vary wildly. So, as you review these numbers think about how you spend money, what your monthly income will be, and what you believe you can afford. 

Average College Costs Include: 

(range: a 2-year college to a private institution)

Tuition - $3,600 - $37,000 

Room and board (meals) - $8,000 - $15,000 

Books and supplies - $800 - $1,500 

Transportation - $500 - $2,000 

Personal expenses - $1,000 - $2,000&nbsp

The Surprise Costs: 

While some costs included on the list below might be considered “standard costs”, there are the smaller, “oh, I forgot about those” costs within the larger category that can bust your budget. 

  • Books and supplies can run anywhere from $500-$1,500 per year depending on your course of study. While you may have calculated one book per class, there may actually be 3 or 4 books required depending on what course you are taking. (algebra may use one large book and a literature course may use 4 or 5 novels) 
  • Transportation costs can sneak up on you. Do you live on or off campus? Do you have a part time job off campus? And, what about those trips home? Think car insurance, gas, car maintenance, parking fees, airplane/bus tickets. 
  • If you choose to live off campus, don’t forget about the utilities that may not be included in rent – water, electricity, internet, food, furniture for the house/apartment, etc. 
  • Personal expenses (haircuts, coffee, toiletries, laundry, eating out, college gear, attending games, etc.) cost approximately $1,000-$1,500 per year
  • Fees/dues (technology, parking, labs, sports, student affairs, Greek life) can run from $1,000-$1,500 per year.  
  • Study abroad, workshops, conventions are very important learning tools for many colleges. That could mean traveling overseas for studying or to local cities for classes that could increase your cost of college from $1,500-$3,000 per year  
  • Technology/computers are required for almost every course and can cost anywhere between $500-$2,000. And, don’t forget maintenance or software update costs.
