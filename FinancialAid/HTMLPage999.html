Various types of Financial Aid:

Types of Federal Aid: 

  • Loans – borrowed money that must be paid back with interest  https://studentaid.gov/understand-aid/types/loans 
  • Grants – federal money that, in most cases, does not have to be repaid  https://studentaid.gov/understand-aid/types/grants 
  • Work Study – part-times jobs allow students to offset college costs  https://studentaid.gov/understand-aid/types/work-study 

Types of Private Scholarships/Grants:

  • Academic/Merit – generally awarded by the college you are attending and are based on your academic performance in high school, many other private scholarships are available  https://www.scholarships.com/financial-aid/college-scholarships/scholarships-by-type/academic-scholarships-and-merit-scholarships/ 
  • Athletic – usually awarded by the college you are attending however, private scholarships do exist 
    http://www.ncaa.org/student-athletes/future/scholarships
    https://collegesofdistinction.com/advice/new-scholarships-for-student-athletes/
  • Need based – based on the ability to pay college costs 
    https://www.unigo.com/scholarships/by-type/need-based-scholarships
  • Community Service – take advantage of your community service during your high school years 
    https://www.scholarships.com/financial-aid/college-scholarships/scholarships-by-type/community-service-scholarships/
  • Personal Background (STEM, heritage, etc.) 
    https://collegesofdistinction.com/advice/2019-list-of-scholarships-for-stem-majors/
    https://www.scholarships.com/financial-aid/college-scholarships/scholarships-by-type/ethnicity-scholarships/
