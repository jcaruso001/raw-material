Author Introduction

Diane Darling, MS, MA, CFT-I™ is a consultant,, author, adjunct professor, and clinical psychology PhD candidate. She holds master’s degrees in psychology and human development and is a Certified Financial Therapy-Level 1™ practitioner. She has extensive clinical training experience working with diverse populations, including military personnel, college students, and community mental health clients. Prior to her doctoral studies, Diane served six years in the U.S. Air Force as a Mandarin Chinese Cryptologic Language Analyst, and has also worked in the field of higher education for several years. Through her experiences, Diane has adeptly combined her military experience, understanding of pedagogy, and psychological insight to contribute significantly to the understanding and well-being of individuals preparing for military service.

Shannon Detone, MA, is currently pursuing her Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology, concentrating her research on empowering underserved populations and addressing racialized trauma. Her academic journey began with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology, where she set the groundwork for her future career in mental health and research. Her dedication to duty and excellence extends beyond her academic pursuits, as evidenced by her military service. She served in the United States Air Force as a military intelligence specialist. Her military service imparted discipline, resiliency, and a profound sense of duty, all of which continue to inform her approach to her research and advocacy work. She actively pursues opportunities for self-improvement and knowledge expansion, exemplifying the notion that lifelong learning is key to personal and professional development.
