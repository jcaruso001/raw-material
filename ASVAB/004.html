III. ASVAB Subtests: Content, Study Strategies, and Time Management

This chapter provides a comprehensive guide to each ASVAB subtest, detailing the content areas, specific study strategies, and time management techniques. This integrated approach helps you understand what to study, how to study it, and how to manage your time effectively during both preparation and the actual test.

General Science (GS)

Content Areas:

The General Science subtest assesses your knowledge across three primary scientific domains:

  • Life Science: This area covers biology, including topics such as cell biology, genetics, evolution, human health, and ecosystems. Understanding basic biological processes and structures is crucial
  • Earth and Space Science: Here, questions focus on geology, meteorology, oceanography, and astronomy. You'll need to know about Earth's composition, atmospheric phenomena, celestial movements, and features of the ocean.
  • Physical Science: This section includes chemistry and physics. Key topics are the structure of matter, basic chemical reactions, laws of motion, energy, and simple machines.

Study Strategies:

  • Use concept maps and visual aids for complex processes.
  • Stay updated with basic principles and recent discoveries through summary articles.
  • Regularly test your knowledge with practice questions.

Time management:

  • You have approximately 11 minutes for 25 questions, so aim to answer each question in about 20-30 seconds.
  • Practice scanning questions quickly and focusing on key words to enhance speed.

Arithmetic Reasoning (AR)

Content Areas:

This subtest is centered around practical arithmetic problems commonly encountered in everyday life, focusing on:

  • Basic Operations: Proficiency in addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
  • Ratios and Proportions: Understanding relationships between different quantities.
  • Percentages: Calculating discounts, interest rates, and other percentage-based figures.
  • Problem Solving: Applying arithmetic to solve real-world problems, requiring an understanding of how to break down word problems and extract the necessary information.

Unlike the Mathematics Knowledge (MK) subtest, the Arithmetic Reasoning subtest is more focused on practical problem-solving using basic arithmetic. It includes word problems that simulate real-life scenarios, requiring logical reasoning and numerical calculations to resolve issues related to finances, measurements, and everyday logistics. AR evaluates your capacity to apply simple arithmetic in practical contexts, testing not just your mathematical skill but also your ability to think through and solve everyday problems efficiently.

Study Strategies:

  • Break down problems into individual steps and solve without skipping processes.
  • Relate problems to real-life situations to enhance understanding.

Time management:

  • With 36 minutes for 30 questions, practice solving problems within a minute, focusing on quickly identifying the necessary operations.

Word Knowledge (WK)

Content Areas:

Word Knowledge evaluates your vocabulary breadth and depth through:

  • Synonyms and Antonyms: Recognizing words that mean the same or opposite.
  • Contextual Usage: Understanding how the meaning of words can change depending on the context in which they are used.
  • Word Roots, Prefixes, and Suffixes: Understanding the building blocks of words can help you deduce the meanings of new words.

Study Strategies:

  • Use flashcards for synonyms and antonyms.
  • Engage with a variety of texts to encounter words in different contexts.

Time management:

  • Given 8 minutes for 16 questions, practice completing each question in less than 30 seconds using rapid review techniques like flashcards.

Paragraph Comprehension (PC)

Content Areas:

This subtest measures your ability to extract information from written text, focusing on:

  • Main Ideas: Identifying the central theme or main idea of a paragraph.
  • Supporting Details: Recognizing facts or details that support the main idea.
  • Inferences: Drawing conclusions based on the information provided in the text.

Study Strategies:

  • Practice active reading strategies such as summarizing and annotating texts.
  • Discuss and explain readings in study groups to reinforce understanding.

Time management:

  • With 22 minutes for 11 questions, aim to spend no more than 2 minutes per question, including reading time. Enhance reading speed through regular practice.

Mathematics Knowledge (MK)

Content Areas:

Mathematics Knowledge tests a range of mathematical concepts:

  • Algebra: Basic algebraic equations and expressions, including variables and linear equations.
  • Geometry: Properties of geometric figures, including area, volume, and the Pythagorean theorem.
  • Data Analysis: Basic statistics such as mean, median, mode, and simple probability.
  • Complex Numbers: Understanding of more advanced algebraic concepts may also be tested.

This subtest differs from the Arithmetic Reasoning (AR) subtest as it delves into high schoollevel mathematics, covering algebra, geometry, and occasionally more advanced topics like trigonometry and basic statistics. It tests your ability to apply mathematical formulas and principles to solve abstract problems, emphasizing a theoretical understanding of mathematics. MK is designed to assess your formal knowledge and ability to handle mathematical concepts in a traditional educational framework.

Study Strategies:

  • Maintain a concise sheet of essential formulas and review them regularly.
  • Solve a variety of problems to familiarize yourself with different question types.

Time management:

  • With 24 minutes for 25 questions, aim to solve each question within about a minute. Practice with timed drills focusing on quick identification of the problem type.

Electronics Information (EI)

Content Areas:

This subtest covers essential concepts related to electronics:

  • Electrical Current: Basics of electrical current, including voltage, resistance, and power calculations.
  • Circuits: Understanding series and parallel circuits, circuit components like resistors, capacitors, and transistors.
  • Devices: Familiarity with common electronic devices and their functions.
  • Electronic Systems: Basic understanding of larger systems like radios, televisions, and computer systems.

Study Strategies:

  • Use diagrams and schematic drawings to understand circuit functions.
  • Watch online tutorials to see electronic concepts in action.

Time management:

  • With 9 minutes for 20 questions, use flashcards to quickly recall electronic principles and enhance response speed.

Auto & Shop Information (AS)

Content Areas:

This subtest splits focus between automotive knowledge and shop information:

  • Automotive Maintenance and Repair: Engine types, brake systems, transmission systems, and routine vehicle maintenance.
  • Wood and Metal Shop Practices: Tools and their specific uses, basic carpentry, and metalworking skills, including measuring and cutting, fastening methods, and safety practices.

Study Strategies:

  • Gain hands-on experience through workshops or classes.
  • Study automotive and shop manuals to understand standard practices.

Time management:

  • With 11 minutes for 25 questions, practice timed photo identification quizzes to speed up tool recognition and decision-making.

Mechanical Comprehension (MC)

Content Areas:

Mechanical Comprehension tests understanding of physical and mechanical principles:

  • Mechanical Devices: Familiarity with devices like gears, pulleys, levers, and inclines
  • Structural Support: Basic engineering concepts related to bridges, buildings, and other structures.
  • Properties of Materials: Understanding different materials' properties, such as density, hardness, and conductivity.

Study Strategies:

  • Learn through examination of everyday mechanical devices.
  • Use models and animations to visualize mechanical principles.

Time management:

  • With 20 minutes for 25 questions, aim for less than a minute per question. Focus on quick problem-solving techniques and visualization during practice.

Assembling Objects (AO)

Content Areas:

This subtest evaluates your ability to understand how different parts fit together spatially:

  • Spatial Orientation: Ability to visualize how parts assemble into a whole.
  • Visual Puzzles: Identifying how different pieces will look when put together, often requiring manipulation of 2D and 3D shapes in your mind.
  • Pattern Recognition: Recognizing and completing patterns and sequences of objects.

Study Strategies:

  • Use model kits or 3D puzzles to improve spatial visualization skills.
  • Practice drawing how parts fit together to better understand assembly instructions.

Time management:

  • With 15 minutes for 25 questions, engage in timed practices that focus on quick visualization and assembly of components.

Summary

Integrating content review, specific study strategies, and effective time management for each subtest of the ASVAB equips you with the tools needed to succeed. By approaching your preparation holistically, you'll be better prepared to manage your time efficiently and perform confidently on test day.
