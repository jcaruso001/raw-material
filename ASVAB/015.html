Subtest 7: Auto & Shop Information (AS)

Time: 11 minutes for 25 questions

Special instructions: Scratch paper allowed. Calculator not allowed. The Auto and Shop Information subtest checks your understanding of automotive maintenance, repair, and basic shop practices, encompassing vehicle and workshop toolrelated topics.

Questions (25 practice questions)

  1. The device responsible for regulating the air-fuel mixture in the engine is known as the
    • A. carburetor.
    • B. radiator.
    • C. distributor.
    • D. alternator.
  2. If a vehicle's steering wheel feels loose and there is difficulty maintaining direction, the issue might be related to a faulty
    • A. water pump.
    • B. power steering pump.
    • C. brake rotor.
    • D. muffler.
  3. The purpose of a timing belt in an internal combustion engine is to
    • A. regulate air intake.
    • B. drive the camshaft and crankshaft in synchronization.
    • C. control fuel injection.
    • D. improve exhaust efficiency.
  4. Which of the following is NOT a function of engine coolant?
    • A. Preventing freezing in cold temperatures.
    • B. Cooling the engine.
    • C. Lubricating engine components.
    • D. Preventing overheating.
  5. A tool used for measuring the gap between two electrical contacts is called a
    • A. micrometer.
    • B. feeler gauge.
    • C. torque wrench.
    • D. vernier caliper.
  6. The purpose of a muffler in an exhaust system is to
    • A. filter out impurities from the exhaust.
    • B. enhance fuel combustion.
    • C. reduce noise produced by exhaust gases.
    • D. regulate tire pressure.
  7. What component of the braking system is responsible for slowing down the rotation of the wheels?
    • A. Brake caliper
    • B. Brake drum
    • C. Brake rotor

  8. The tool used to measure the voltage of a car battery is called a
    • A. tachometer.
    • B. voltmeter.
    • C. ammeter.
    • D. ohmmeter.
  9. In a fuel-injected engine, the component that meters the amount of air entering the engine is the
    • A. throttle body.
    • B. carburetor.
    • C. intake manifold.
    • D. exhaust manifold.
  10. The component responsible for igniting the air-fuel mixture in a gasoline engine is the
    • A. spark plug.
    • B. glow plug.
    • C. fuel injector.

  11. Overheating the engine can cause all of the following problems EXCEPT
    • A. burned engine bearings.
    • B. enlarged pistons.
    • C. melted engine parts.
    • D. improved fuel efficiency.
  12. The device that converts an automobile’s mechanical energy to electrical energy is called the
    • A. converter.
    • B. alternator.
    • C. battery.
    • D. brakes.
  13. A primary advantage of the electronic ignition system over conventional ignition systems is that
    • A. the electronic ignition system is less expensive to repair.
    • B. the electronic ignition system requires a lower voltage to provide a higher voltage for spark
    • C. the electronic ignition system allows for use of a lower octane fuel.
    • D. all of the above
  14. The primary purpose of piston rings is to
    • A. seal the combustion chamber and allow the pistons to move freely.
    • B. connect the piston to the crankshaft.
    • C. allow fuel to enter the piston cylinder.
    • D. provide lubrication to the piston cylinder.
  15. The crankshaft typically connects to a
    • A. flywheel.
    • B. fuel pump.
    • C. muffler.
    • D. battery.
  16. What component allows the left and right wheels to turn at different speeds when cornering?
    • A. differential
    • B. camshaft
    • C. valve rotator
    • D. battery
  17. If a car’s ignition system, lights, and radio don’t work, the part that’s probably malfunctioned is the
    • A. cylinder block.
    • B. water pump.
    • C. carburetor.
    • D. battery.
  18. A good tool to cut intricate shapes in wood would be a
    • A. ripsaw.
    • B. hacksaw.
    • C. coping saw.
    • D. pocket knife.
  19. A two-stroke engine will normally be found on
    • A. small cars.
    • B. large diesel trucks.
    • C. trucks, vans, and some cars.

  20. A belt sander would best be used to
    • A. cut wood.
    • B. finish wood.

  21. What component in an automobile is primarily responsible for initiating the ignition process?
    • A. Alternator

  22. Which tool is used to check the alignment of wheels on a vehicle?

  24. What is the function of a catalytic converter in an automobile?
    • A. To convert exhaust gases into less harmful substances.
    • B. To enhance fuel efficiency by recycling exhaust gases.
    • C. To control the flow of exhaust gases out of the engine.

  25. Identify the tool used to measure the thickness of brake pads in an automotive brake system.
    • A. Caliper
    • B. Micrometer
    • C. Vernier caliper
    • D. Feeler gauge

  2. B. power steering pump.

  6. C. reduce noise produced by exhaust gases.
  7. A. Brake caliper.
  8. B. voltmeter.
  9. A. throttle body.
  10. A. spark plug.
  11. D. improved fuel efficiency.
  12. B. alternator.
  13. B. the electronic ignition system requires a lower voltage to provide a higher voltage for spark.
  14. A. seal the combustion chamber and allow the pistons to move freely.
  15. A. flywheel.
  16. A. differential.
  17. D. battery.
  18. C. coping saw.
  19. D. snowmobiles, chainsaws, and some motorcycles.
  20. B. finish wood.
  21. C. Starter motor
  22. B. Alignment gauge
  23. B. The vehicle turns more than the amount commanded by the driver
  24. A. To convert exhaust gases into less harmful substances
