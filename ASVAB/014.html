Subtest 6: Electronics Information (EI)

Time: 9 minutes for 20 questions

Special instructions: Scratch paper allowed. Calculator not allowed. The Electronics Information subtest examines your knowledge of electrical principles, basic electronics, and circuitry, covering electricity, circuits, and electronic systems.

Questions (20 practice questions)

  1. What is the function of a resistor in an electronic circuit?
    • A. To store electrical energy.
    • B. To control or limit the flow of electrical current.
    • C. To convert electrical energy into mechanical energy.
    • D. To amplify an electrical signal.
  2. What unit is used to measure electric current?
    • A. Volt
    • B. Ampere
    • C. Ohm
    • D. Watt
  3. In a direct current (DC) circuit, how does the current flow?
    • A. From the positive to the negative terminal.
    • B. From the negative to the positive terminal.
    • C. In alternating directions.
    • D. It does not flow; it oscillates.
  4. What is the primary purpose of a capacitor in an electronic circuit?
    • A. To regulate voltage.
    • B. To store and release electrical energy.
    • C. To convert AC to DC.
    • D. To increase signal strength.
  5. Which component is typically used to produce light in an electronic circuit?
    • A. Resistor
    • B. Transistor
    • C. Light Emitting Diode (LED)
    • D. Inductor
  6. What is the purpose of a diode in an electronic circuit?
    • A. To store electric charge.
    • B. To resist the flow of current.
    • C. To allow current to flow in only one direction.
    • D. To amplify signals.
  7. Which electronic component is used to increase the strength of an electrical signal?
    • A. Resistor
    • B. Capacitor
    • C. Transistor
    • D. Inductor
  8. What is Ohm's Law?
    • A. Voltage equals current times resistance.
    • B. Current equals voltage divided by resistance.
    • C. Resistance equals voltage divided by current.
    • D. Power equals voltage times current.
  9. In a parallel circuit with identical bulbs, what happens if one bulb burns out?
    • A. All bulbs will go out.
    • B. Remaining bulbs will shine brighter.
    • C. Remaining bulbs will continue to work normally.
    • D. The circuit will short out.
  10. What is the unit of measurement for electrical power?
    • A. Ohm
    • B. Ampere
    • C. Volt
    • D. Watt
  11. What electronic component can be used to store charge for a short period?
    • A. Resistor
    • B. Capacitor
    • C. Transistor
    • D. Diode
  12. What is the primary function of a transformer?
    • A. To convert DC to AC.
    • B. To store electrical energy.
    • C. To change the voltage of an electrical current.
    • D. To allow current to flow in one direction only.
  13. What does the term 'semiconductor' refer to?
    • A. A material that fully conducts electricity.
    • B. A material that has no electrical conductivity.
    • C. A material that conducts electricity under certain conditions.
    • D. A component that converts electricity into light.
  14. What is a multimeter used for in electronics?
    • A. To measure current, voltage, and resistance.
    • B. To increase signal strength.
    • C. To store electrical energy.
    • D. To connect different components.
  15. What property of a circuit does an ammeter measure?
    • A. Voltage
    • B. Resistance
    • C. Current
    • D. Power
  16. In electronics, what is a PCB?
    • A. Printed Circuit Board
    • B. Power Control Box
    • C. Programmable Circuit Breaker
    • D. Pulse Code Modulation
  17. Which of the following is a common use for a silicon-controlled rectifier (SCR)?
    • A. Amplifying audio signals
    • B. Storing electrical energy
    • C. Switching and controlling high-power devices
    • D. Converting AC to DC
  18. What does 'AC' stand for in electrical terms?
    • A. Actual Current
    • B. Alternating Current
    • C. Amplified Current
    • D. Advanced Circuitry
  19. Which component is used to protect electronic circuits from excessive current?
    • A. Resistor
    • B. Fuse
    • C. Capacitor
    • D. Transformer
  20. What characteristic of a wave is measured in Hertz (Hz)?
    • A. Amplitude
    • B. Speed
    • C. Frequency
    • D. Wavelength

Answers

  1. B. To control or limit the flow of electrical current.
  2. B. Ampere.
  3. A. From the positive to the negative terminal.
  4. B. To store and release electrical energy.
  5. C. Light Emitting Diode (LED).
  6. C. To allow current to flow in only one direction.
  7. C. Transistor.
  8. A. Voltage equals current times resistance.
  9. C. Remaining bulbs will continue to work normally.
  10. D. Watt.
  11. B. Capacitor.
  12. C. To change the voltage of an electrical current.
  13. C. A material that conducts electricity under certain conditions.
  14. A. To measure current, voltage, and resistance.
  15. C. Current.
  16. A. Printed Circuit Board.
  17. C. Switching and controlling high-power devices.
  18. B. Alternating Current.
  19. B. Fuse.
  20. C. Frequency
