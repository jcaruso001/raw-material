Subtest 4: Paragraph Comprehension (PC)

Time: 13 minutes for 15 questions

Special instructions: Scratch paper allowed. Calculator not allowed. The Paragraph Comprehension subtest focuses on your ability to understand and interpret written material, testing reading comprehension and critical thinking.

Questions (15 practice questions)

  1. "The Eiffel Tower, a symbol of Paris and a marvel of 19th-century engineering, was initially met with skepticism by critics. Designed by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 World's Fair, it was deemed an eyesore by some of the city's artists and intellectuals. However, over time, the iron lattice tower has become not only a globally recognized icon but also a symbol of the ingenuity and artistry of its era."

    What was the initial public opinion about the Eiffel Tower among Parisian intellectuals?

    • A. They admired it for its ingenuity.
    • B. They were skeptical and viewed it as an eyesore.
    • C. They were indifferent.
    • D. They considered it a symbol of national pride.

  2. "Photosynthesis is a process used by plants to convert light energy into chemical energy. This energy is stored in the form of sugars, which are created from water and carbon dioxide. The process not only sustains plant life but also forms the basis of the food chain by providing essential nutrients to herbivores."

    What is the primary function of photosynthesis in plants?

    • A. To produce oxygen for the atmosphere.
    • B. To convert light energy into chemical energy.
    • C. To absorb water from the soil.
    • D. To provide shade and shelter for animals.

  3. "In 1969, the Apollo 11 mission marked a significant milestone in space exploration by landing the first humans on the Moon. Astronauts Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin walked on the lunar surface, while Michael Collins orbited above in the command module. This historic event was watched by millions around the world and is often regarded as one of humanity's greatest achievements."

    Who were the astronauts that walked on the Moon during the Apollo 11 mission?

    • A. Michael Collins and Buzz Aldrin
    • B. Neil Armstrong and Michael Collins
    • C. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin
    • D. John Glenn and Buzz Aldrin

  4. "The Great Barrier Reef, located off the coast of Queensland, Australia, is the world's largest coral reef system. Comprising over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands, it spans over 2,300 kilometers. The reef is known for its biodiversity, including numerous species of fish, mollusks, and birds, and is a popular destination for tourists and researchers."

    What is the Great Barrier Reef primarily known for?

    • A. Its length and distance from the coast.
    • B. Its biodiversity and size.
    • C. Being a popular destination for researchers.
    • D. Its location near Queensland, Australia.

  5. "The human brain is a complex organ responsible for intelligence, senses, movement, and behavior. Consisting of billions of neurons, the brain processes information received through the senses, making decisions, and coordinating actions. It is divided into several regions, each with specific functions, making it the control center of the body."

    What is the primary function of the human brain?

    • A. To pump blood throughout the body.
    • B. To process information and control bodily functions.
    • C. To aid in physical movement.
    • D. To regulate breathing and heart rate.

  6. "The Amazon Rainforest, often referred to as the 'lungs of the Earth,' plays a critical role in the global climate. It is a vast region that encompasses a variety of ecosystems and is home to an immense diversity of wildlife. The rainforest is vital for absorbing carbon dioxide and producing oxygen, thus maintaining the Earth's ecological balance."

    Why is the Amazon Rainforest crucial for the Earth's ecological balance?

    • A. It provides habitat for a variety of wildlife.
    • B. It is a popular tourist destination.
    • C. It absorbs carbon dioxide and produces oxygen.
    • D. It is the largest rainforest in the world.

  7. "The theory of plate tectonics explains the movement of the Earth's lithospheric plates. These plates move over the asthenosphere and are responsible for many geological phenomena, including earthquakes, volcanic activity, and the formation of mountain ranges. The theory revolutionized the understanding of Earth's geological history."

    What does the theory of plate tectonics primarily explain?

    • A. The composition of the Earth's core.
    • B. The movement of the Earth's lithospheric plates.
    • C. The process of erosion and sedimentation.
    • D. The evolution of terrestrial life forms.

  8. "The Industrial Revolution, a period of rapid industrial growth during the late 18th and early 19th centuries, began in Britain and soon spread to other parts of the world. This era saw the introduction of machinery, which drastically changed manufacturing processes, and led to significant social, economic, and cultural changes."

    Where did the Industrial Revolution begin?

    • A. The United States
    • B. Germany
    • C. Britain
    • D. France

  9. "Lightning is a natural electrical discharge of very short duration and high voltage between a cloud and the ground or within a cloud. The phenomenon is accompanied by the emission of light and typically occurs during thunderstorms. Lightning is a powerful and unpredictable natural force that can cause significant damage."

    What is lightning?

    • A. A type of sound wave.
    • B. A natural electrical discharge.
    • C. A chemical reaction in the atmosphere.
    • D. A form of ultraviolet radiation.

  10. "Polar bears, native to the Arctic region, are well adapted to their cold environment. They have thick fur and a layer of fat to insulate them from the cold, and large paws that help them navigate across ice and snow. As carnivorous animals, their diet primarily consists of seals."

    What adaptations help polar bears survive in the Arctic?

    • A. Their ability to fly short distances.
    • B. Their thick fur and layer of fat.
    • C. Their nocturnal hunting habits.
    • D. Their preference for a plant-based diet.

  11. "The Renaissance was a period of great cultural and intellectual growth in Europe, spanning from the 14th to the 17th century. It marked the transition from the Middle Ages to modernity and is characterized by achievements in art, literature, and science. The movement began in Italy and spread to other parts of Europe, leading to significant developments in various fields."

    What was the Renaissance?

    • A. A political movement in 18th-century Europe.
    • B. A cultural and intellectual growth period in Europe.
    • C. A war that took place in the 14th century.
    • D. An economic downturn in Italy.

  12. "The theory of relativity, proposed by Albert Einstein, transformed the understanding of space, time, and gravity. It consists of two parts: the special theory, which addresses the relationship between space and time, and the general theory, which describes the force of gravity as the warping of space-time around massive objects."

    What is the theory of relativity primarily about?

    • A. The structure of atoms.
    • B. The relationship between space, time, and gravity.
    • C. The laws of thermodynamics.
    • D. The composition of the universe.

  13. "The human circulatory system is a complex network that functions to transport blood and nutrients throughout the body. It includes the heart, blood vessels, and blood. The heart acts as a pump, circulating blood through a series of vessels that reach every part of the body, delivering oxygen and nutrients to cells and removing waste products."

    What is the primary function of the human circulatory system?

    • A. To produce red blood cells.
    • B. To transport blood and nutrients throughout the body.
    • C. To filter toxins from the body.
    • D. To aid in digestion.

  14. "The Great Depression was a severe worldwide economic depression that took place mostly during the 1930s. Originating in the United States, the depression had devastating effects globally, particularly in industrialized countries. It was marked by significant declines in industrial production, widespread unemployment, and deflation."

    What was the Great Depression?

    • A. A global health crisis in the 1930s.
    • B. A severe worldwide economic depression in the 1930s.
    • C. A political movement in the United States.
    • D. An environmental disaster in industrialized countries.

  15. "Photosynthesis and cellular respiration are two fundamental biological processes. Photosynthesis, occurring in plants, converts light energy into chemical energy, producing oxygen as a byproduct. Cellular respiration, occurring in both plants and animals, uses oxygen to release energy from food. These processes are crucial for sustaining life on Earth."

    How are photosynthesis and cellular respiration related?

    • A. Both produce light energy.
    • B. Both occur exclusively in animals.
    • C. Both use oxygen to release energy from food.
    • D. Photosynthesis produces oxygen, which is used in cellular respiration.

Answers

  1. B. They were skeptical and viewed it as an eyesore.
  2. B. To convert light energy into chemical energy.
  3. C. Neil Armstrong and Buzz Aldrin.
  4. B. Its biodiversity and size.
  5. B. To process information and control bodily functions.
  6. C. It absorbs carbon dioxide and produces oxygen.
  7. B. The movement of the Earth's lithospheric plates.
  8. C. Britain.
  9. B. A natural electrical discharge.
  10. B. Their thick fur and layer of fat.
  11. B. A cultural and intellectual growth period in Europe.
  12. B. The relationship between space, time, and gravity.
  13. B. To transport blood and nutrients throughout the body.
  14. B. A severe worldwide economic depression in the 1930s.
  15. D. Photosynthesis produces oxygen, which is used in cellular respiration.
