Military Options

Here is short introduction to the components or branches of the U.S. Armed Forces and their Service Academies.

  • The Army is our largest component – the ground-gaining force. It is made up of the Active Duty, Reserve, and National Guard units. The Army’s service academy is the U.S. Military Academy aka West Point located in West Point, New York.
  • The Air Force is similar in make-up Active, Reserve, and National Guard components and is the air-controlling element of our armed forces. The Air Force Academy is located in Colorado Springs, Colorado.
  • The Navy is our sea service component with active and reserve forces. The U.S. Naval Academy is located in Annapolis, Maryland.
  • The U.S. Marine Corps employs troops on land and by sea and it has active and reserve units. The USMC uses the U.S. Naval Academy to produce officers.
  • The U.S. Coast Guard, with active and reserve components, is part of our Homeland Defense with a mission to guard our borders and waterways. The U.S. Coast Guard Academy is in New London, Connecticut.
  • The Merchant Marines primarily transports cargo and handles imports and exports vital to our economy during peacetime. In time of war it can be an auxiliary to the U.S. Navy. The U.S. Merchant Marine Academy in Kings Point, New York trains officers to serve in the United States Merchant Marine, branches of the military, and the transportation industry.
