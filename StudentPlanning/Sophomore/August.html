Sophomore Year: August - September

Financial literacy is essential for college success and beyond. Build your money management skills with free resources from the USAA Educational Foundation.

If you are just beginning the college prep process, please review the Freshman Year section and complete the work there before proceeding.

ACTIVITY: Take the Jung-Myers Briggs Personality Typology Test http://www.humanmetrics.com/cgi-win/JTypes2.asp

This activity will help you discover how your personality reflects your life, school, college, and future career possibilities and choices.

  • Review college preparatory curriculum with your school counselor and/or parents and assess current course schedule.
  • Assess academic strengths and weaknesses. Remember:
    • Colleges look for Foreign Language courses with a minimum of two years.
    • Most colleges prefer a full load of Foreign Language, English, History, Science, and Math each year of high school.
    • Rigor is extremely important.
  • Continue building your portfolio.
  • Review original goals you set freshman year.
  • Now is the time to select extracurricular activities in which to get more involved.
    • Think about taking on leadership roles—leadership skills are an asset that colleges consider heavily in the admissions process.
  • Talk to your school counselor about registering and preparing to take the PSAT.
    • This test is usually scheduled in October and will be taken at your school. Find out from your counselor where and how to sign up.
    • Review key points from either “The Essential Guide to the ACT” or “The Essential Guide to the SAT” or both.
    • Though the PSAT is not a test score that will be considered by colleges, the test is based on and organized like the real ACT and SAT and provides excellent practice for the actual test you will take in your junior year.

What Next?

  • Meet with your high school counselor to review and discuss your 4-year plan.
  • Update as necessary, then review and discuss your progress with your parents.
  • Review “The Essential Guide to the ACT” and/or “The Essential Guide to the SAT” to prepare for the PSAT. Take a practice test to prepare for this standardized test.
