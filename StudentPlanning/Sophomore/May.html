Sophomore Year: May

Briefly review “What Next?” from spring.

Review Military Options - You may or may not have considered joining the military, but it is an important option to consider when thinking of paying for college. The military offers many options for financial educational assistance.

  • Military Academies - If you are considering a future at a military academy, be aware of the competition, but also understand the benefits that attending an academy offers when being a part of the military.
  • Reserves/Guard/ROTC - Understand the benefits of these programs regarding tuition assistance, fee waivers, GI Bill options, loan repayment programs, part-time work options, and scholarships.
  • Active Duty - Enlistment in the Armed Forces
    • Realize and understand that this may not be the best action to take if you want to go directly to college after completing high school.
    • Enlisting requires a 2-6 year commitment of active duty service.
    • Enlisting can offer tremendous monetary assistance and future life skills.
  • Senior Military Colleges and Military Junior Colleges - There are numerous military colleges to research for where to attend college while being within the military environment and profession.


ACTIVITY: Investigate the Reserves/Guard/ROTC, Active Duty, and Military Colleges to create a list comparing the similarities and differences each one offers.

Summer Plans

  • Seek out summer opportunities.
    • Jobs, volunteer positions, internships, activities
    • Speak to your high school counselor about summer school programs offered at colleges for high school students.
  • Begin a summer reading list. It is always better to keep as much knowledge fresh as possible.
