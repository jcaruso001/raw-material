Interview Skills

  • Preparation for a job or internship interview
    • You should have some knowledge about the organization, group, or company with which you will potentially be interning before the interview.
    • Research online to find out more about where you are interviewing and arrive prepared with questions about the position or company.
  • Creating an Effective Resume - Review examples of effective resumes and address common mistakes. Prepare a resume which includes academics, athletics, honors, awards, clubs, organizations, and positions held, and your academic or educational goals statement. Use the following resources to help draft your resume:
  • https://www.thebalancecareers.com/high-school-student-resume-example-2063279
  • https://resumegenius.com/resume-samples/high-school-resume-example
  • https://resumecompanion.com/resume-examples/high-school-student-resume-sample/
  • Be aware of the common mistakes that occur in interview settings before they happen!
  • Time Management - the day of the interview is an important day to ensure that you are on time. This is not the day to be late if that is common for you, or to try an unknown “shortcut” to the interview, or to grab a bite to eat on the way. First impressions are important, and it is not wise to be running behind for that first impression. Arriving 10 minutes early is considered on time.
  • Importance of proper language skills - in an ever-changing English vocabulary, slang and acronyms are increasingly popular; however, proper grammar, sentence structure, and appropriate language are required to make a good impression on your potential employer.
  • Apparel
    • Propriety and modesty are important when deciding what to wear to an interview.
    • Find out if the interview is casual, business casual, or business attire in order to dress appropriately.
    • Ladies: if you wear a dress or skirt, be sure to be conscious of how you are positioned throughout the interview.
  • Good Posture – make a point to stand and sit up straight
  • Rehearse the “Yes/No Sir/Ma’am” rule


ACTIVITY: Mock Interview

Find an adult who can act as the potential employer and practice the skills discussed above to improve future interviews.
