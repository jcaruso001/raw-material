Senior Year: December

Briefly review the “What Next?” from November.

Appeals and Alternate Plans

  • If you have been rejected by one of your top college choices, there is the option to go through an appeals process.
    • Be sure to talk to an admission counselor and tell him or her that you are appealing.
    • Explain to the Admission Office why you should be accepted.
  • If all else fails, you can always take the community college road first. Complete your first year and make great grades, proving that you are qualified to attend a 4-year college.

Appealing Award Letters

  • If you plan to appeal an award letter because you believe you deserve a greater amount, be sure to express this concern before you attend the college or university.
    • Explain a lack of income or other reasons why you think you deserve a greater amount.
    • Do not bring up other school offers for awards.

Selecting a Major

  • Identify your areas of interest in terms of study and career to help narrow down a career and education path.
  • Research degree requirements for careers of interest to you.
  • Determine the amount of outside class preparation time is required for different majors of interest.
  • Match your ambitions with a desired degree field of interest. What interests you about that type of degree? For example, if you are interested in science, what do you prefer? Chemistry? Biology? Physics?


Selecting the Right College

ACTIVITY: Using all your previously gathered information and acceptance letters, create a pro/con list of the schools at which you’ve been accepted to narrow down where you want to attend!

What Next? If you wish to go through the appeals process, now is the time to do so.

Staying on Track...

  • Search and apply for scholarships that are due within the next month for seniors.
  • Plan your class schedule for next semester.
  • Over break, update your high school portfolio as necessary.
  • Prepare your Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) as soon as the form is available (usually October 1).
