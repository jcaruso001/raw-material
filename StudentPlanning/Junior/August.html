Student Pre-College Planning Guide:

  • Review your class schedule with your high school counselor and/or your parents, making sure classes are challenging and in line with graduation and college admissions requirements.
  • This is the year your classes will carry significant weight. Your grades will either help or hurt you.
  • Use this time to make up some ground if you have fallen behind at all in classes. Really stretch and challenge yourself with a strong, rigorous course load.
  • Continue building your high school work and achievements portfolio.
  • Seek opportunities for leadership roles in extracurricular activities.
  • Develop and maintain good study habits and time management skills and make wise social choices—including social media.
  • Consider joining the Academic Team if your school offers one.
  • Consider taking the ASVAB as a career assessment tool. This test is administered in November.

ACTIVITY: Review dates for the ACT and SAT and plan when you will take one or both tests. Remember, you may take the tests more than once, so allow additional time if you want to try to improve your score. Be sure to take at least one practice test before taking the ACT or SAT. Review “The Essential Guide to the ACT” and/or “The Essential Guide to the SAT” prior to upcoming tests.

ACTIVITY: Begin attending local and regional college fairs, especially if your top schools will be at these events. Also, check your high school calendar for visits from college representatives.
