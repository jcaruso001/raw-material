More Keys to Winning Scholarships

Search scholarships for free

Never pay anyone for scholarship searches or scholarship money. If you are paying for scholarship money, that should serve as a warning that something is amiss. There are plenty of free databases available, such as those listed previously in this unit.

Think small

Be sure to take advantage of possible funding available in your community. Don’t rule out your local organizations such as PTA’s, Lions Clubs, VFA, community foundations, and other groups. The applicant pool for this funding will be smaller, and it will be easier to win a scholarship than it will be when competing for scholarships from a large national organization. The scholarship/grant award amounts may not be as significant, perhaps $1,000 or less – but they add up!

For a clear understanding of how to “think small”, look at the following website; http://money.cnn.com/2003/01/10/pf/college/sat_grants/

Don’t Wait

Start applying for scholarships early. Most scholarships have specific timeframes for applying. For assistance with scheduling, see our Student Planning Guide.

Continue applying for scholarships when you are in college

Don’t assume that if you have already started college scholarships are not available.

From your freshman year of college continuing through graduate school, people are willing to help you pay for school so that they can get professionals on the backside of that college education.
