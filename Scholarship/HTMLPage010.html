Keys to Winning Scholarships

Be Involved

Involvement is the key to both scholarships and entry into college. Seek out opportunities to lead, volunteer, or participate in every endeavor. Some high schools require volunteer hours for graduation, and documented volunteer/service hours are also sometimes required for scholarship eligibility. Add volunteering to your college planning so you avoid having to accomplish service hours near the end of your high school tenure. (See the College Planning Guide section for details). Click here for the Student Planning Guide

Know Your Data

For the most part, scholarship applications are generic. In other words, once you have the standard data required for one scholarship, you have the data for many. Acquire documentation from your school such as GPA, SAT/ACT scores, class ranking, list of extracurricular activities, letters of recommendation, etc. in order to complete applications.  

Know Your Interests

Consider what you want to pursue in college, your favorite activities and/or interests, then search according to your list. Being awarded a few small scholarships can really add up. 

Establish Routines

Set aside one or two hours per week to work on the scholarship process.  

Be Organized

Bookmark the websites you have searched. Create a spreadsheet of scholarships you are applying to (website, deadline, type of scholarship, etc). Keep this information throughout college as you can continue applying for scholarships during those years.
