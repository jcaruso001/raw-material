Chapter Three: Pronoun Usage

We already talked about pronoun/antecedent agreement, but you must also know when to use which pronoun case (such as I versus me). You must use nominative pronouns (such as I, he, and they) for subjects and predicate nominatives. (Jacob and I are studying for our math test.) You must use objective pronouns (such as me, her, and them) for direct objects, indirect objects, and objects of prepositions. (Will you eat lunch with Rudy and me?) Most students have trouble deciding when to use who and when to use whom. Remember that who (nominative case) is for subjects (in both independent and dependent clauses) and predicate nominatives. Whom(objective case) is for direct objects, indirect objects, and objects of prepositions. Also, remember that hisself, theirselves, and themself are not words.
