Writing and Language

The writing and language portion of the SAT involves responding to multiple-choice questions. And they’re tough ones at that! Therefore, only the most grammar-savvy students should face this section of the test without some grammar review.

The multiple-choice questions in this section are of two types. The first involves the usage and mechanics of Standard English. The portion of the passage to which the questions refers is usually underlined with a number under the underlined portion. The second type involves issues of rhetoric or structure. These include such things as sentence combining, identifying appropriate word choice, sentence sequencing, paragraph sequencing, determining author’s purpose, and style. These questions generally refer to the passage by placing the question number in a box close to the corresponding section. The SAT contains a much larger percentage of rhetorical questions than does the ACT.
