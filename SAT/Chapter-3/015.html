Chapter Three: Sentence Fragments

A sentence fragment is a sentence that isn’t complete. In order to be complete, a sentence must be a complete thought with a subject and a verb. Beware of sentences that start with subordinating conjunctions (like if, because, since, and although). A clause that starts with a subordinating conjunction is dependent and cannot stand alone as a sentence, so be sure that you have another clause—an independent one—following the dependent one.
