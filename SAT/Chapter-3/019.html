Idioms

An idiom is a word or expression that cannot be taken literally. For example, “He pulled the wool over my eyes.” Unfortunately (or perhaps fortunately!), there are no rules to learn about idioms. They either sound right, or they don’t. The most common idiom errors you’ll come across, though, are preposition errors. For example, it would be incorrect to say, “He pulled the wool across my eyes.”
