Chapter Four: Percent Increase/Decrease

We mentioned above that we cannot solve a problem with a percent that is still in percent form. Another important rule to remember is that percentages are never by themselves. Percentages tell us how much of something there is so a percent must always be followed by the something they are telling us about. This is important for percent increase and decrease problems.

Example 1: Tira’s salary was $20,000 last year. This year her salary is being increased by 25%. What is the amount of Tira’s salary for this year?

We are starting with $20,000 and then adding 25% more. But 25% more of what? The answer is 25% more of her salary. Remember, “of” means “multiply”.

20,000+.25×20,000
=20,000+5,000
=25,000

Tira’s salary this year is $25,000.

Example 2: The original price of a jacket was $45. During the spring sale, the jacket was advertised for 40% off. What is the sale price of the jacket?

We are starting with $45 and then taking away 40%. But taking away 40% of what? We are taking away 40% of the price.

45-.40×45
=45-18
=27

The sale price of the jacket is $27.
