Chapter Four: Algebraic Expressions and Manipulation

What’s With All the Letters? Algebraic Expressions and Manipulation

Many of the questions on the SAT math test may not include numbers at all. These questions (and others) will test your knowledge of basic algebraic concepts. You will need to be comfortable working with algebraic expressions, equations, and functions.

Definitions and Basics

An algebraic expression is a mathematical expression that consists of variables, numbers, and operations. For example:

3xy + y/x – x 

Is an expression.

Terms are made up of numbers or variables combined by multiplication or division. 3xy is a term in the above algebraic expression as well as y/x and x.

In order to achieve success on the exam, you will need to be able to apply the basic operations of arithmetic to algebraic expressions.

For example, like terms (terms containing the same variables that are combined in the same way) can be added and subtracted. When adding and subtracting like terms, you add or subtract the numbers in front of each term called coefficients:

4x + 5x = 9x
5a + 6b + 2a – 3b = 7a + 3b
15x – 3y – (− 6y) + 7x − 5 = 22x + 3y – 5
