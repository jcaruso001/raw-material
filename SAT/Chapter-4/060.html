The graph of a quadratic function is called a parabola. A parabola is a U-shaped curve that can open upward or downward depending on the sign of a. If a > 0, then the graph will open upward. If a < 0, then the graph will open downward.


In the equation above, c is the y-intercept of the function. The parabola will cross the y-axis at c. When matching a quadratic equation to its graph, it is very helpful to look at a and c. The sign of a will tell you the orientation of the graph, whether it opens upward or downward. The value of c will tell you where the parabola crosses the y-axis. These two pieces of information are typically enough to identify the corresponding graph of the function.
