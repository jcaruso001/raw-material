Percents

A fraction with a denominator of 100 can be rewritten as a percent and vice versa. The word “percent” literally translated means “out of 100”.

99/100=99%

23/100=23%

7/100=7%

3/100=3%

Percents can also be rewritten as decimals. The decimal form of a percent is often the most useful when solving problems involving percents. You can never solve a problem when a percent is in percent form (%). You must convert to either a fraction or a decimal.

99%=99/100=.99

23%=23/100=.23

7%=7/100=.07

3%=3/100=.03

To quickly convert from a percent to a decimal, move the decimal from behind the number in the percent form two places to the left to get the correct decimal form. To convert from a decimal to a percent, move the decimal point in the decimal number two places to the right.

50%=.50
.02=2%

When solving percent problems, it is important to remember the three keys: “of” means “multiply”, “out of” means “divide”, and “is” means “equals”.

Example: What is 30% of 20?

First, we need to convert 30% to a fraction or a decimal. Let’s use the decimal form, .3. Next, remember that “of” means “multiply”.

.3×20
= 6

Example 1: In the survey, 2 out of every 5 people prefer candidate A. What percent prefer candidate A?

Remember, “out of” means “divide”.

2/5=.4=40%

Example 2: When the restaurant first opened, they had only 40 diners a night. 6 months later, the restaurant serves 90 diners a night. What is the percent increase in diners after the first 6 months?

We first need to determine the amount of increase.

90 – 40 = 50

The restaurant now serves 50 diners out of the original 40.

50/40=1.25=125%

After the first 6 months, there was a 125% increase in the number of diners in the restaurant.
