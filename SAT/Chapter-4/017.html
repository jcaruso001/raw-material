Chapter Four: Unit Conversions

Canceling out factors comes in handy when we are trying to convert between different units. This is called unit convers or dimensional analysis. Suppose we want to convert 2 yards to inches. Our main goal is to cancel out yards and replace them with inches. We can do this with fractions.

We start be considering the fact that we have 2 yards and reason our way to inches. We know that there are 3 feet for every 1 yard. We also know that there are 12 inches for every 1 foot.

We can now cross cancel the common units until we are only left with inches.

Example: Andrew is riding his bike and his speed is 20 miles per hour. How fast is he going in feet per second? (1 mile = 5280 feet)

We are starting with 20 miles per hour (20mi/1hr ). Now we have to reason our way from miles to feet and hours to seconds. There are 5280 feet in 1 hour. There are 60 minutes in 1 hour and 60 seconds in 1 minute. Remember, in order to cross cancel, we need to set up the fractions so that the units are opposite from each other.

We can now cross cancel the common units so we are left with only feet in the numerator and seconds in the denominator.
