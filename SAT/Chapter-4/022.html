Chapter 4: Definitions for Algebraic Exponents

Exponents, also known as powers, signify how many times a term is multiplied by itself.

x^3 = x • x • x

Key concepts to remember:

• Add exponents when multiplying expressions with the same base:

x^3• x^4 = (x • x • x)(x • x • x • x) = x^7

x^a + x^b = x^a + b

Negative exponents signify how many times the reciprocal of a term is multiplied by itself.

a^-4 = 1/a • 1/a • 1/a • 1/a = 1/ 4

Any term raised to an exponent of 1 is equal to itself.

x^1 = x

Any term other than zero raised to an exponent of zero is equal to 1.

x^0 = 1

Solving Equations

This is one of the most important components of the exam. Algebraic expressions will appear in many different forms, ranging from simple linear equations to complex word problems. Some questions may ask you to solve for a specific value of x, while others may ask you to solve for x in terms of (an)other variable(s).
