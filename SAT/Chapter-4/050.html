Similar and Congruent Triangles

Two triangles are similar if their corresponding angles have the same measure. The triangles will have the same shape, and their sides will be proportional. For example, the two triangles below are similar. Both are 45°- 45°- 90° right triangles; therefore, the sides of the first triangle are proportional to the sides of the second triangle.


We can determine the value of any side of one of the similar triangles by using a proportion containing a ratio of the unknown side and any other side from either of the two triangles.

For example, consider two triangles with sides lengths 2, 3, 5 and 6, 9, x respectively. Let’s solve for x.

First, we need to construct a proportion using a ratio of the unknown side and any other side from either of the two triangles and a second ratio constructed in the same way.

x/ 5 = 9/3

Next, we cross multiply and solve for x.

3x = 45
x = 15

Two triangles are similar if:

  1. Two pairs of corresponding angles have the same measure.
  2. One pair of corresponding angles has the same measure, and the pairs of corresponding sides that form those angles have lengths that are in the same ratio.

Congruent triangles are triangles that have the same size and shape.


Each side of ∆ABC has the same length as the corresponding side of ∆DEF.

AB = DE = x
BC = EF = y
CA = FD = z

Each angle of ∆ABC is equal to its corresponding angle in ∆DEF.
