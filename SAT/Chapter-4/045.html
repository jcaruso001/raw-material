Graphical Displays of Data

Data is often displayed in graphs. There are several different types of graphs that can be used.

Bar graphs are used to help visualize how different parts of the data compare to each other and help organize large sets of data.


Each bar shows how many rainy days there were for each month. From the graph we can easily tell things such as which month had the most or least days of rain, the average number of rainy days per month, and what seasons are the driest. Remember, we can only answer according to the information given in the graph. Even if we know something to be true, if it is not shown in the graph, that information cannot be a part of our answer. This is important! Pretend that you have forgotten everything about life and only know the information that the graph provides. This will help keep you from being distracted by answers that are true statements but are not based on the graph.

Line graphs are another good way to display data. They are very helpful for seeing how data changes.

From this line graph we can see that the person started walking at a steady rate away from home. After 10 minutes, her distance from home stayed the same so she must have stopped walking. At 15 minutes she starts walking again, this time faster than before. At 25 minutes she turns around and starts slowly walking home.

Circle graphs or pie graphs are usually displayed with percentages. In order to find the value of each slice we will need to find the percent of the whole.

Example: What is the degree measure for the slice of the pie graph that represents the second quarter?

The second quarter holds 20% of the data so the degree measure will be 20% of the circle. There are 360° in a circle and 0.2×360=72. So, the degree measure for the slice of the pie graph that represents the second quarter is 72°.

Data can also be organized in a table.

Example: The table above shows the sale prices of the houses in a certain area. What is the median sale price?

Remember, the median is the number in the middle after the data has been ordered from least to greatest. In this case we need to keep in mind how many houses of each price there are.

< $200,000 (130,500), $200,000-$300,000 (250,000), $300,000-$400,000 (375,200), >$400,000 (150,000)

To find the amount that is in the middle, we need to work from the outside inward. We start by taking 130,500 houses off of each end.

$200,000-300,000 (250,000), $300,000-400,000 (375,200), >$400,000 (19,500)

Now we take 19,500 houses off of each end

$200,000-300,000 (230,500), $300,000-400,000 (375,200)

Now we take 230,500 houses off each end (We always take away the smaller of the two end values)

$300,000-$400,000 (144,700)

Since $300,000-$400,000 is the only category left, the median must be in that category. Thus, the median sale price is $300,000-$400,000.

Another very common graphical display of data is the scatter plot. A scatter plot displays points comprised of two separate sets of data about the same objects or events. The scatter plot helps us see how the two sets of data correlate and helps us predict values not included on the graph.


We can see from this plot that the points move in the positive direction in a linear pattern. Thus the data has a positive linear correlation. We can show this correlation better with the line of best fit. The graph below shows the same data points with the line of best fit included.


The line of best fit will have the same number of points above and below the line.

Example: What is the equation of the line of best fit?

From the graph it looks like the line contains the points (50, 52) and (55, 57).


Since we cannot really tell what points are actually on the line, our equation is just a very good estimate for the actual line of best fit (y = 0.9979x + 1.474). Since the test is multiple choice, our estimate should be enough to lead us to the correct answer.

Example: What is the expected quiz grade for Chemistry when the Algebra quiz grade is 65?

All we have to do is plug in our Algebra quiz grade into the line of best fit.

y = 1(65) + 2
y = 65 + 2
y = 67

The expected Chemistry quiz grade would be a 67.
