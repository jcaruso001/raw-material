Chapter Four: Solving Absolute Value Equations and Inequalities

Familiarity with both the concept and notation of absolute value will be helpful in solving math questions. The absolute value of a number is its distance from zero on the number line. The absolute value of the number x is denoted |x|. For example, | 10 | = 10 and | − 5 | = 5. The absolute value of a number is always positive since it represents the distance.

You will be expected to work with expressions and solve equations and inequalities that involve absolute value.

Example: Solve | 2x – 3 | > 5.

Since we are working with absolute value, we have to consider both the distance in the positive direction and the negative direction. Consider this example:

| 2x – 3 | > 5

For our expression to have a distance greater than 5 in the positive direction, the expression will have to be greater than 5. For our expression to have a distance greater than 5 in the negative direction, the expression will have to be less than -5. The first thing to do is to split the inequality into two:

2x – 3 < – 5 or 2x – 3 > 5

Then solve for x in each equation.

2x < – 2 or 2x > 8 x < – 1 or x > 4

Thus, the solution to | 2x – 3 | > 5 consists of all values of x that satisfy x < – 1 or x > 4.
