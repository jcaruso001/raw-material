The Quadratic Equation

Some of the quadratic equations on the SAT are not easy to factor. This is typically the case when there is a coefficient in front of the x^2 term that cannot be factored out of every term. In this case, you will need the quadratic equation to solve for the solutions. Let’s assign letters to each of the coefficients in a quadratic equation.

ax^2 + bx + c

To solve for x we can use the quadratic formula.


Example: Find the solutions to the equation 2x^2+13x+15=0.

The 2 does not factor out of the entire equation which makes this difficult to factor. Thus, we should use the quadratic equation. Start by listing out the values of a, b, and c.

a = 2, b = 13, and c = 15

Now we plug these values into the quadratic equation and simplify.


Thus we have the two solutions


The solutions to the quadratic equation 2x^2+13x+15=0 are x = -3/2 and x = −5.

Solving inequalities works just like solving an equation, except for one important difference: When multiplying or dividing an inequality by a negative number, you must flip the inequality sign.

Example 1: Solve the inequality 2x – 5 > 9.

First, add 5 to both sides to give 2x > 14.

Then solve for x by dividing both sides by 2. The solution is x > 7.

This expression means that all values of x greater than 7 are solutions to this inequality.

Example 2: Solve the inequality 3x + 5 < 5x – 9.

First, subtract 5 from both sides to give 3x < 5x – 14.

Then subtract 5x from both sides to give – 2x < – 14.

To solve for x, divide both sides by – 2 and flip the inequality sign. The solution is x > 7.

You may also be given graphical representations of inequalities.

Example 3: Which of the following algebraic inequalities is represented by the graph below?


A. – 2 ≥ y < 2
B. – 2 < y > 2
C. – 2 ≤ y < 2
D. – 2 < y ≤ 2

The graph represents a line whose solution, y, lies between – 2 and 2. The filled point above -2 shows that y can be equal to -2. The line runs from the filled point to the right. Thus, we know that -2≤ y. The open point above 2 shows that y cannot be equal to 2. The line runs from the open point to the left. Thus, we know that y < 2. To answer the question, you must combine the two inequalities into one statement:
– 2 ≤ y < 2. Therefore, the answer is C.
