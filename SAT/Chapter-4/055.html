Circles

A circle represents all the points at a distance of r away from any given point. The distance, r, is called the radius. In the circle below, the center is at O. The measure of degrees around the point of O is 360°.

Definitions

  • A chord is a line connecting two points on a circle

    The diameter of a circle is any chord that passes through the center of the circle. The diameter is the largest chord in the circle. Chord BD is a diameter of circle 0.
  • The radius of a circle is any line segment extending from the center of the circle to a point on the circle. All radii of the same circle have equal length

    The radius is also half the length of the diameter.
  • A tangent to a circle is a line that touches the circle at only one point. A tangent is always perpendicular to the radius whose endpoint is the point of contact between the tangent line and the circle. AC is a tangent to circle O.
  • The circumference is the measure of the distance around the circle. It is equal to π times the diameter, d (or π times twice the radius, r).

    Circumference = πd
    or
    Circumference = 2πr

  • An arc is a part of the circumference of the circle. An arc can be measured in degrees or in units of length. If you form an angle by drawing radii from each end of the arc to the center of the circle, the degree measure of the angle is equal to the degree measure of the arc.

    The area of a circle is equal to π times the square of the radius.

    Area of a circle = πr^2
