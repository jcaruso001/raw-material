Chapter Four: Multiplication of Fractions


When multiplying a series of fractions, it helps to recognize factors that you can cancel prior to multiplication. 

1/3 x 2/5 x 3/4 x 5/7

We can cancel the 3 and the 5 because they appear in both the numerator and the denominator. We can even cancel the 2 in the numerator with a factor of 2 from the 4 in the denominator.

By canceling these factors prior to multiplication, we arrive at an answer that is in reduced form. Had we not done this and just multiplied across, we would have had to reduce 30/420.
