Solving Radical Expressions

The expression 2√x is a radical expression because it involves a root square, in this case, the root of x.

To simplify radical expressions, break down each factor under the radical into perfect squares. Then Take the square root of the perfect squares and multiply the results. Whatever is not a perfect square remains in the radical.

The equation 2√x + 7 = 19 is radical because it involves a radical expression.

You can solve this equation in the same way you solved other linear equations:

2√x + 7 = 19
2√x = 12
√x = 6

In order to get x out from under the radical, you will need to square both sides of the equation.

x = 6^2
x = 36
