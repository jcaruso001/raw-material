Finding the Equation of a Line

We can find the equation of any line when given two points on that line.

Example: What is the equation of the line that passes through the points (2, 4) and (3, 7)?
We start by writing down the formula for a line.

y = mx + b

We need to find the values of m and b. We will start with m, the slope.


Now we need to find the value of b. In order to solve for b we need to get rid of x and y in the equation. Since the points (2, 4) and (3, 7) are on our line, we know that when x = 2, y = 4 and when x = 3, y = 7. We can plug in either of these pairs of values into the equation for x and y and then solve for b. Let’s use (2, 4).

4 = 3(2) + b
4 = 6 + b
−2 = b

Thus, the equation of the line that passes through the points (2, 4) and (3, 7) is:

y = 3x −2

Sometimes the questions on the SAT have a built-in shortcut. Occasionally the question starts by telling us that the slope is 3 and then gives us the point (2, 4). In this case we can skip the first step of finding the slope and jump right to the final step of solving for b.

Below is the graph of the line y = 2x + 1. The line crosses the y-axis at a point 1 above the origin (y = 1). The slope is 2. An easy way to think of the slope is as the fraction 2/1, where 2 represents the direction of movement on the y-axis and 1 represents the direction of movement on the x-axis. Thus, from y = 1 on the y-axis, you would move up 2 and right 1.


In the graph above, it is clear that the value of the y-intercept is indeed 1 since the line crosses the y-axis at the point (0, 1). It is important to note that the x-coordinate of the y-intercept will always be equal to 0 since the point is on the y-axis. Now take a look at where the line crosses the x-axis, the x-intercept. The x-intercept occurs at the point (− 1/2, 0). The y-coordinate of the x-intercept will always be equal to 0 since the point is on the x-axis. We can easily double check the value of the x-intercept by plugging 0 in for y in the equation for the line.

y = 2x + 1
0 = 2x + 1
−1 = 2x
− 1/2 = x

If the equation of a line is y = –3/2 x – 1, you would start by moving 1 down from the origin to y = –1. The slope is negative so you would move 3 down and 2 to the right to give you the graph shown below.


Key relationships

  • A positive slope represents an inclining line (y values increase from left to right).
  • A negative slope represents a declining line (y values decrease from left to right).
  • Parallel lines have equal slopes. For instance, the lines y = 3x + 1 and y = 3x – 2 are parallel lines because both lines have a slope of 3.
  • Two lines are perpendicular when the product of their slopes equals –1. The lines y = 2x + 1 and y= -1/ x – 3 are perpendicular lines because the product of their slopes equals –1
