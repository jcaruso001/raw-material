Graphs of Trigonometric Functions

You will be expected to be able to identify the graph of a trigonometric function from a given equation or to find an equation from a given graph.

The general equation for a given trigonometric function is represented by (shown below for the
sine of angle θ)

y = asin(kθ),

Where a is the amplitude of the graph (the distance from the middle of the graph to the tallest peak) and k modifies the period of the graph. The period of the graph is the distance between one point to its corresponding, repeated point. The period of the graph always has a value of 2π/k units.
An increase in k results in a decrease in the period (an inverse relationship).

Below are the graphs of the simple functions y = sin x and y = cos x, respectively:

y = sin x


The amplitude of the graph is 1 because sin x = 1sin x. Also, we can see from the graph that the distance from the middle of the graph to the tallest peak is 1. The period is 2π because sin x = sin 1x. Thus k = 1 and 2π/1 = 2π. Looking at the graph if we pick the point (0, 0), its matching point is (2π, 0) which is a distance of 2π.

y = cos x


Here the amplitude and period are also 1 and 2π.
Looking at the graphs of sin(x) and cos(x) we can tell that
sin(0) = 0 
sin(π/2) or sin(90) = 1 
sin(π) or sin(180) = 0 
sin(2π/3) or sin(270) = −1

cos(0) = 1
cos(π/2) or cos(90) = 0
cos(π) or cos(180) = 1
cos(2π/3) or cos (270) = 0

These values are important to remember if a calculator is not available.

You should also be familiar with the graph of tan x. The tangent function does not have an amplitude.
